**1. Obscure Survivors**
The preflight is a sampling artifact as evidence of *existence*, but a real flaw as evidence of *quality*. The sample is alphabetical and tiny, and the build log admits all survivors have negative scores and weak bridges ([docs/lbd-build-log.md](/C:/Users/wijesingheds/Documents/04%20Fun%20with%20Coding/2026-07-07%20PyZoBot-Arbiter/docs/lbd-build-log.md:81)). The raw JSON confirms this: top survivors are ABCD2/AAMDC/AAK1 with scores around `-1.0` to `-1.4`, despite being ?disjoint.?

The scoring formula is the problem: `z(ab)+z(bc)-w*ac_lit-w2*ac_known` ([src/arbiter/lbd/cooccur.py](/C:/Users/wijesingheds/Documents/04%20Fun%20with%20Coding/2026-07-07%20PyZoBot-Arbiter/src/arbiter/lbd/cooccur.py:29)). It does not explicitly reward obscurity, because high `ab` is supposed to rescue only biologically bridged genes, but it *does* make `ac_lit=0` too cheap and lets broad disease popularity dominate via `bc`, which is constant per disease across all genes ([src/arbiter/lbd/cooccur.py](/C:/Users/wijesingheds/Documents/04%20Fun%20with%20Coding/2026-07-07%20PyZoBot-Arbiter/src/arbiter/lbd/cooccur.py:50)).

Fix: score bridged novelty as a gated/balanced objective, not a linear reward. Require `ab` above a full-universe percentile, use `min(z_ab, z_bc)` or `sqrt(log1p(ab)*log1p(bc))`, penalize `log1p(ac_lit)` not raw `ac_lit`, and add a ?not obscure? prior: dataset effect strength or minimum gene-program/T-cell literature count. Otherwise top hits will include genes that are novel because no one wrote about them.

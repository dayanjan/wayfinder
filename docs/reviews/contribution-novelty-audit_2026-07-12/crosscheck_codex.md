# Codex cross-adjudicator (Phase D, captured from stdout log)

F1 — REFINE. M1 remains technically distinct as an exact integration, but “NOVEL” overstates its methodological distance from prior work. Huang/Zou (arXiv 2502.09858) already addresses the same LLM-hypothesis-overproduction problem through data-grounded sequential falsification with error control and evaluation. That collapses the broad claim that falsification-based data adjudication fills an unoccupied gap. Wayfinder’s surviving differentiators are the deterministic referee, reuse of a held pre-existing Perturb-seq substrate, LBD triple structure, and per-hop receipts/QC abstention. That is a specific implementation/integration novelty, not a new methodological category. It could support a methods contribution only if its benefit were measured; without evaluation, the conjunction alone is insufficient. The manuscript must cite and compare Huang/Zou directly.

F2 — CONFIRM. The manuscript explicitly says the workflow is “demonstrated, not evaluated at scale” (§5.3), that the ledger is “not a measured precision or recall” (§4.1), and that there is “no external validation and no precision or recall against a ground truth” (§5.3b). It therefore establishes correct execution and computational reproducibility, not decision quality. For a conventional Frontiers-family methods research article, this is a major and probably rejection-level deficiency, especially because the closest neighbor reportedly includes controlled evaluation. It is not universally fatal if recast as a methods-and-practice/perspective note, but it is fatal to claims of an evaluated prioritization method.

F3 — REFINE. The evidence establishes that 12q13 is a longstanding atopy locus, STAT6 has strong independent allergic-disease biology, and no retrieved study positively nominated NAB2. It therefore supports “NAB2 may be an LD passenger and STAT6 is the leading alternative.” It does not establish that NAB2 is *likely* a passenger or conclusively that STAT6 is the causal 12q13 driver. The verifier itself overstates the prioritization evidence: its appendix says Sobczyk did not list STAT6 among its single candidates, while the 2023/2025 abstracts merely do not name NAB2. Abstract-level absence cannot prove absence from full locus tables or supplements. The manuscript’s hedge is adequate: the abstract explicitly says the linkage-inherited label cannot be discharged, and §§4.4b/5.3 deny a settled NAB2–eczema link. B2 is thin and unresolved, but not materially overstated as written.

F4 — CONFIRM, with calibrated wording. Fifteen targeted searches found no direct NAB2–Th1/Th2, NAB2–GATA3, or NAB2–Th2-cytokine report, while the retrieved NAB2 literature concerns activation, anergy, CD8 responses, and thymic homeostasis. That supports “novel-for-NAB2 in the surfaced literature,” not the absolute “genuinely absent from all literature.” The nomination was mechanistically foreseeable: NAB2 regulates EGR-family factors, and Singh et al. directly places EGR2/3 in suppression of Th1 differentiation under Th2/Th17 conditions. Thus it is a plausible one-hop extension, neither already reported nor surprising. The EGR2/3 adjacency should be acknowledged explicitly.

F5 — CONFIRM. The cis-test is competent and appropriately scoped. NAB2 knockdown is strong, while STAT6 is essentially unchanged at the tested Stim8hr aggregate expression readout (log₂FC +0.087, adjusted p=0.788; rank 5,444/10,282). The manuscript correctly treats the 43 kb geometry as suggestive rather than decisive and recognizes that the CRISPRi-spreading literature does not establish 43 kb as a safe boundary. Its conclusion—no detectable STAT6 expression-level cis effect at this readout—is supported. It does not exclude transient, subset-specific, chromatin-level effects or resolve the separate LD disease-label confound.

CROSS-MODEL VERDICT: I agree with the adversaries’ bottom line: do not publish the current manuscript as a biological discovery or as an evaluated methods paper; major revision could make it a useful methods-and-norms note. My main dissent is narrower than theirs: the retrieved evidence does not justify calling NAB2 a *likely* LD passenger or STAT6 conclusively the locus driver—only a serious, unresolved alternative. Likewise, Huang/Zou does not erase Wayfinder’s exact deterministic/held-substrate/per-hop integration, but it reduces that novelty to an unevaluated implementation distinction. The strongest surviving contributions are the NAB2-specific perturbation nomination, the disciplined abstention rule, and the well-scoped STAT6 artifact test—not predictive validation or a new eczema mechanism.

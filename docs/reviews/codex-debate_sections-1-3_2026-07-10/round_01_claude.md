# Round 1 — Claude's opening position (prose debate, sections 1-3)

## Framing question
Are the drafted sections 1-3 the strongest, most honest, most reviewer-proof PROSE for an FRMA
methods+demonstration paper — sound argument, consistent framing (approach-not-product; two receipt
classes), calibrated language, surviving a hostile LBD-community reviewer?

## The position I am defending
1. **The argument is: LBD overproduces and validates almost nothing; an agentic loop that generates,
   adjudicates against a held substrate, and self-audits closes the *triage* gap.** The Introduction earns
   the R01 critique as its hinge and the "two floods" as its motivation; Background teaches both audiences;
   Methods specifies the generator, the 4-hop referee, and the workbench.
2. **The prose keeps the two receipt classes distinct** — experimental for KD/effect/program, association
   (GWAS-genetic nomination) for disease — and never lets the disease claim inherit experimental weight.
3. **The approach-not-product stance is consistent** — Wayfinder is a method; only Claude Science is a
   "workbench/product."
4. **The confident NO + untested-not-negative** is stated as the moat (§3.3 hop 0 + the demotions).
5. **The no-API → headless UI automation from Claude Code** (§3.4) is presented as a distinctive,
   honestly-caveated route to reproducible agentic science.
6. **Language is calibrated** — a prior number+language pass flagged only one word ("oracle"), now fixed;
   "demonstrates" (not "proves"), "nomination" (not "discovery").

## What I most want stress-tested
- **The strongest reviewer attack.** A hostile FRMA/LBD reviewer's single best objection to sections 1-3
  as written — is it "the agentic headline over-reaches for a text-mining venue," "the program-hop
  tautology undercuts the 4-hop framing," "retrospective adjudication isn't really 'follow-up'," or
  something I haven't pre-empted?
- **Prose overclaim I've gone blind to** — any sentence where the drafting inflated beyond what the
  method supports, especially in the Introduction's motivation and the §3.4 self-audit paragraph.
- **Framing leaks** — any sentence where "approach" slips back to "product", or the disease hop is
  described with experimental weight, or the CS self-audit is implied to be cross-vendor.
- **Argument gaps** — does §2 actually motivate why a *held experimental substrate* is the right test
  (vs. classic time-slicing), or does it assert it? Does §3 justify the objective's design choices, or
  just state the formula?
- **preserve-intent both ways** — did any earlier calibration leave a real strength *under*-stated?
